## Format
12 to 20 minute compressed historical timelines. Long-form (16:9 landscape), single continuous
narrative walking a historical arc at a compressed pace. Single narrator voice throughout, not
multi-character voice acting.

## Visual Approach — Three Layers (agreed at concept level)
Every scene combines three layers in the same frame:
1. **Real historical photography base layer.** The factual, period-accurate ground of the frame.
   Real photographs, not illustration. (Sourcing/licensing and the real-person overlap under Part 4
   are unresolved, see ChannelD/CLAUDE.md.)
2. **Vector map / text overlay layer.** Factual anchoring, clean vector maps, routes, dates, place
   names, figures. Sits over the photography base to orient the viewer in time and place.
3. **Flat 2D cartoon character overlay layer.** Comedic reaction beats. Flat, simple 2D cartoon
   characters layered on top of the real base for humor, the tonal contrast (real history + cartoon
   reaction) is the channel's signature device.

## Composition (concept-level intent)
- **16:9 landscape**, built for long-form watch-on-TV/desktop as much as mobile.
- Real photography fills the frame as the base; vector map/text anchors sit in consistent, readable
  positions (a recurring "anchor zone" so the factual layer is predictable across the timeline).
- Cartoon reaction characters occupy a consistent overlay zone so the comedic layer reads as a
  deliberate device, not visual noise. The tonal contrast between real base and cartoon overlay is
  the point; keep the two layers visually distinct, never blended into one style.
- The three layers must stay legible together: base does not overwhelm text, cartoon does not bury
  the base.

## Layout / Pacing (concept-level)
- Compressed timeline pacing: the narration drives, visuals cut to keep a long runtime moving.
- Vector map/text anchors appear at each new date/place shift to reset the viewer's orientation.
- Cartoon reaction beats punctuate, they do not run continuously, roughly one comedic overlay per
  narrative turn, not every scene (final cadence to be tuned once the pipeline exists).

## Captions
- Lower-third, clean high-contrast sans, inside a landscape safe zone. Exact typeface TBD.

## Thumbnail Formula (PROPOSED, not yet decided)
Based on the described three-layer identity. Mark as proposed until Banc confirms:
- Real historical photograph as the base, instantly period-readable.
- A single flat 2D cartoon reaction character overlaid on it, exaggerated expression, carrying the
  comedic hook.
- One vector text/date anchor element to signal the "history, compressed and fun" promise.
- Bold high-contrast title text. Emotional read: curiosity plus a comedic wink, the tonal contrast
  is the sell.
