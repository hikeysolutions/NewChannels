# Channel E — "Fish Outta Water" — Style Guide

> Status: PLACEHOLDER. Character design has not started, and this channel is sequenced last on
> purpose because its build order runs in reverse: character design → visual style → everything
> else (see ChannelE/CLAUDE.md). The visual direction below is concept-level intent only. The
> defining specifics (the character, the locked look) do not exist yet, so this is deliberately
> thin. Fill it out only once character design is underway.

## Format
30 to 60 second shorts (vertical 9:16). Wholly original character(s), no real people, no existing
IP, dropped into high-stakes, absurd, premium-aesthetic scenarios: VIP events, reality-competition
chaos, high-roller nightlife.

## Visual Identity (concept-level intent, not locked)
- **Cinematic / luxury** aesthetic. High-contrast lighting is the one firm directional note.
- Premium, aspirational scenario staging (the "fish out of water in a world of excess" premise
  carries the comedy: an ordinary/original character against a high-roller backdrop).
- **Consistency-as-authenticity is the visual mandate, not just an aesthetic choice.** The single
  hardest problem here is parasocial attachment to a brand-new character (ChannelE/CLAUDE.md). The
  answer is the character staying visually identical across every appearance until the audience
  treats that consistency as a real identity. Visual consistency is therefore the top style
  priority, above any individual scene's polish.

## Composition / Layout (to be defined after character design)
- Vertical 9:16, subject-forward framing, high-contrast lighting separating the character from a
  premium background.
- Detailed composition, layout zones, and safe-zone rules: TBD once the character exists. There is
  nothing concrete to specify before the character is designed.

## Thumbnail Formula (PROPOSED, not yet decided)
Based on the described luxury identity. Mark as proposed until Banc confirms and until the character
exists to anchor it:
- The original character front-and-center, unmistakable expression, in an obviously premium/luxury
  setting (the contrast between the character and the setting is the hook).
- High-contrast cinematic lighting, character cleanly separated from the opulent background.
- Consistent character presentation across every thumbnail (the recognizability compounds, this is
  the whole strategy).
- Emotional read: aspirational absurdity, "how did they end up here."
