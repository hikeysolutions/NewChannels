# Channel C — "FailNation" — Style Guide

> Persona note: FailNation's narration/comedic voice spec is not yet written. It will live in

## Format
30 to 60 second comedic "fail" shorts. Vertical short-form (9:16). Fast, one gag per clip, built to
land on a single clean comedic beat. Slapstick physics, safe resolution, subjects always unharmed.

## Content Scope (LOCKED — governs every still and clip)
Young adults and animals only. No minors. No real, identifiable people. No existing/copyrighted IP.
Every fail resolves safely. See ChannelC/CLAUDE.md for the full locked scope and the uncensored-tier
hard rule.

## Composition
- **Vertical 9:16**, single dominant subject centered or slightly off-center, framed for a phone.
- **Clear before/after gag geometry.** The setup reads instantly (subject + the thing about to go
  wrong), the payoff is unmistakable. Camera holds wide enough to show the full slapstick physics,
  never so tight the gag is ambiguous.
- **High-contrast lighting** on the subject so the comedic beat pops on a small screen and in a
  fast-scrolling feed. Subject clearly separated from background.
- **Character/style consistency across the sequential stills** is the priority GPT Image 2 was
  chosen for. Same character build, face, and garment type must hold across every still in a clip.
  Do not rely on the low quality tier for exact color continuity of any single element if a gag
  depends on that color staying identical (see CLAUDE.md / Part 5).

## Layout
- Subject occupies the central vertical third; leave headroom top and bottom for safe-zone captions
  and platform UI (the bottom ~15% is covered by YouTube Shorts UI, keep the gag out of it).
- Backgrounds support the premise but never compete with the subject for attention.
- Motion is the payload: the still-to-clip step carries the physical gag, stills set it up.

## Captions
- Punchy, short, comedic. Large high-contrast sans, centered or lower-third, inside the safe zone.
- One readable line at a time, timed to the beat. Never a paragraph on screen.
- Exact typeface: TBD (lock alongside color theme).

## Thumbnail Formula (PROPOSED, not yet decided)
Based on the described visual identity. Mark as proposed until Banc confirms:
- Single dominant subject caught mid-fail, peak comedic expression, exaggerated body language.
- The "about to go wrong" moment frozen at its most readable, the viewer instantly gets the joke
  setup without the payoff.
- High-contrast lighting, subject cleanly separated from background.
- Optional very short punch-word overlay (one or two words), not a full title.
- Emotional read: comedic anticipation / secondhand cringe, never real distress.
